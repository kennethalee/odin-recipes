Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert known for its rich layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, velvety mascarpone cream, and a dusting of cocoa powder. The delicate balance of flavors, combining the boldness of espresso with the creamy sweetness of mascarpone, creates a sophisticated yet indulgent treat. Perfect for special occasions or as a luxurious everyday dessert, tiramisu embodies the art of Italian culinary elegance.

Ingredients

  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 2 large eggs (separated)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup strong brewed espresso (cooled)
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 package ladyfinger biscuits (savoiardi)
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Prepare the espresso: Brew strong espresso and let it cool. Mix with coffee liqueur if using.
  2. Whisk egg yolks: In a bowl, whisk egg yolks with sugar until pale and creamy. Add mascarpone and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth.
  3. Whip egg whites: In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gently fold into the mascarpone mixture for a light, airy texture
  4. Dip the ladyfingers: Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the cooled espresso mixture, ensuring they are coated but not soggy.
  5. Layer the tiramisu: In a rectangular dish, arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture evenly over them. Repeat with another layer of ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.
  6. Dust with cocoa: Generously sift cocoa powder over the top layer.
  7. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to let the flavors meld.
  8. Serve chilled and enjoy the rich, creamy goodness of tiramisu!
