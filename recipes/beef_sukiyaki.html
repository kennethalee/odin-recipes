Beef Sukiyaki

Description

Beef Sukiyaki is a classic Japanese hotpot dish that combines thinly sliced beef with an assortment of vegetables, tofu, and noodles simmered in a sweet-savory broth made from soy sauce, sugar, and mirin. Served in a communal pot, sukiyaki brings warmth and comfort, perfect for sharing with family or friends. The tender beef and hearty vegetables absorb the flavorful broth, creating a harmonious balance of umami and sweetness, often accompanied by a raw egg for dipping.

Ingredients

  • 300g thinly sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • 1 block firm tofu, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 bunch green onions, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 cup napa cabbage, cut into strips
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms (shiitake or enoki), trimmed
  • 1 package shirataki noodles, drained
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the broth:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup water

Optional:

  • 2 eggs, beaten for dipping

    • Steps

    1. Prepare the broth: In a bowl, mix soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and water until sugar dissolves. Set aside.
    2. Heat the pan: Heat a wide, shallow pot or skillet over medium heat and add vegetable oil.
    3. Cook the beef: Add a few slices of beef to the pot, searing them lightly before pushing them to the side.
    4. Add vegetables and tofu: Arrange tofu, green onions, napa cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, and shirataki noodles around the beef in the pot.
    5. Pour the broth: Pour the prepared broth evenly over the ingredients.
    6. Simmer and cook: Cover and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the flavors are absorbed.
    7. Serve: Transfer the pot to the table, allowing diners to pick their portions. Dip cooked ingredients into raw beaten egg (if desired) for added richness.
    8. Enjoy your flavorful and comforting beef sukiyaki!
