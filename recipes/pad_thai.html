Pad Thai

Description

Pad Thai is a popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish that balances the perfect blend of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. Made with rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, scrambled eggs, tofu, and bean sprouts, it's tossed in a tangy tamarind-based sauce and garnished with crushed peanuts, lime, and fresh herbs. Pad Thai is a quick and flavorful meal that embodies the vibrant and rich culinary traditions of Thailand.

Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp or chicken (optional), peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 100g firm tofu, diced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons crushed peanuts
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles: Soak rice noodles in hot water according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the sauce: Mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and chili powder in a bowl. Adjust sweetness or sourness to taste.
  3. Heat the pan: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  4. Cook the protein: Add shrimp or chicken to the skillet and stir-fry until cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  5. Scramble the eggs: Add the beaten eggs to the skillet and stir quickly to scramble.
  6. Stir-fry the tofu: Stir-fry the tofu
  7. Add noodles and sauce: Return the protein to the skillet and add the noodles. Pour the sauce over and toss until well coated.
  8. Add vegetables: Stir in bean sprouts and green onions. Cook for 1-2 minutes until slightly wilted.
  9. Garnish and serve: Transfer to plates, sprinkle with crushed peanuts, and serve with lime wedges on the side.
  10. Enjoy your delicious homemade Pad Thai!
